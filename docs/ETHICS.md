# CODEX Ethics and Scholarly Guardrails

## Purpose
CODEX is interpretive software for exploratory symbolic analysis. It maps recurring metaphors and motifs into a structured analytical vocabulary. It does **not** determine doctrine, settle historical disputes, or establish metaphysical truth.

## Core Guardrails
- Outputs are hypotheses generated from deterministic lexical rules.
- Results should be reviewed by human readers with context and domain knowledge.
- The project aims to respect source traditions and avoid dismissive or appropriative framing.
- We avoid reductionism: symbolic overlap does not erase differences in theology, history, language, or ritual practice.
- We avoid universalizing claims such as "all traditions are the same."
- We encourage collaboration with scholars, practitioners, and tradition holders.

## Communication Rules
- Use conservative confidence language tied to explicit evidence.
- Distinguish clearly between detected phrase overlap and interpretive conclusions.
- Never present model output as scientific proof or historical certainty.

## Data and Source Discipline
- Include only short sample excerpts suitable for demonstration.
- Do not bundle copyrighted texts without permissions.
- Preserve provenance metadata as corpus support grows.
